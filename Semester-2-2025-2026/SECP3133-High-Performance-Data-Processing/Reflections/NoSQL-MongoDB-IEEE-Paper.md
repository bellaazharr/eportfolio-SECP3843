# Reflection — Academic Writing: NoSQL Databases & MongoDB (IEEE Format)

## Objective
Produce an IEEE-style conference paper on NoSQL databases and MongoDB, meeting academic writing standards through detailed rubric-based self-review.

## What I Did
Beyond researching and writing the technical content, a significant part of this assignment was the self-review process — going through the rubric line by line and checking my own draft against it before submission, then correcting issues I found rather than waiting for feedback.

## What Was Difficult
Writing in IEEE format with academic rigor was a different skill than writing a typical report — every claim needed to be precise and ideally backed by a citation, and the structure (abstract, related work, methodology, etc.) had to be followed exactly. The rubric-based self-review was also harder than I expected, because it meant being genuinely critical of my own writing instead of assuming it was already clear.

## How This Connects to Course Objectives
This taught me that academic writing about a technical topic — like NoSQL's trade-offs versus relational databases — is itself a high-performance data processing skill: communicating dense technical information efficiently and accurately, in a fixed, constrained format.

## What I'd Do Differently
I'd do the rubric-based self-review earlier in the writing process, not just at the end, since some structural issues I found late would have been easier to fix if caught before the paper was nearly complete.
