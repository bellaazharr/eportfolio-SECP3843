# Reflection — SkinAqua Business Intelligence Project

## Objective
This group project asked us to take a business problem through the full BI workflow — data preparation in Alteryx, visualization in Tableau, and finally translating both into insights a non-technical stakeholder could act on. My responsibility was Phase 3: insights and business recommendations.

## What I Did
By the time the dataset reached me, it had already been cleaned and structured through the team's Alteryx workflow. My job was to look at what the Tableau visualizations actually showed and turn that into recommendations — not just "the chart shows X," but "given X, the business should consider Y."

## What Was Difficult
The hardest part was resisting the urge to write generic, safe-sounding recommendations. It's easy to write "the company should invest more in marketing" — it's much harder to write a recommendation that's specifically justified by a pattern in the data. I had to go back to the visualizations multiple times to make sure each recommendation actually traced back to a specific insight.

## How This Connects to Course Objectives
This phase is where BI architecture closes the loop — all the earlier work on data sources, ETL, and dashboard design only matters if it leads somewhere a business can use. It reinforced that the "I" in BI is the recommendation, not the dashboard.

## What I'd Do Differently
I'd want earlier visibility into the Alteryx workflow logic itself, not just its output, since a couple of times I wasn't fully sure why a metric was calculated the way it was.
