# Overall Course Reflection — SECP3106

## What I Gained
Working on the E-ducator project with a real client (IKM Johor Bahru) taught me that application development in a team is mostly a communication problem wearing a technical disguise. The schema mismatches I debugged in Module 4 weren't really Flutter or Supabase problems — they were the result of the backend contract not being communicated clearly across the team as features were built in parallel.

## Suggested Improvement
I'd want the course to spend more time explicitly on team workflow practices (schema documentation, branch conventions) rather than assuming students will work them out under deadline pressure, since that's where most of my actual debugging time went.

## Personal Note
Setting up Flutter from scratch on Windows and getting through early environment issues was frustrating at the time, but it's the kind of foundational friction that made every later feature easier to build.
