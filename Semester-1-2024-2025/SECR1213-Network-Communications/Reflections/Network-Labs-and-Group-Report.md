# Reflection — Network Communications (Labs & Group Report)

## Objective
Build practical understanding of networking concepts through hands-on labs and a group report/task.

## What I Did
Completed individual labs progressively covering networking fundamentals, plus contributed to a group report and a related task (6a/6b).

## What Was Difficult
Networking concepts are easy to memorize in the abstract (OSI layers, protocols) but harder to reason about when something doesn't work as expected in a lab setting — diagnosing *why* a configuration failed required actually understanding the layer it touched, not just naming it.

## How This Connects to Later Coursework
This course's emphasis on tracing a problem to the correct layer of a system is a pattern I've seen repeat in data engineering: knowing which "layer" (ingestion, transformation, network, etc.) an issue actually belongs to is most of the debugging work.

## What I'd Do Differently
I'd document my troubleshooting steps during labs in real time, rather than only writing up the final working solution — the reasoning in between is what I'd want to look back on.
